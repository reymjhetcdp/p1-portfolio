How to ace your Presentation!

Hi, I’m Reymjhet Cunanan and I have conducted an interview for this assessment. I was able to interview a graduating student about how her final undergraduate research defense unfolded. At first, she said that she didn’t know if she could pull off her defense, but surprisingly, all her hard work paid off as her panelists were quite impressed with her presentation. I was able to learn a lot from her.

So, without further ado, here are the tips and tricks I gathered that will help you ace your next presentation.

Before presenting, of course, you need to know what type of presentation you’re giving. This will determine how you should speak and act in front of your audience. If you’re giving a thesis defense presentation, then you need to be formal with your words and actions. If it’s a webinar, you can be flexible. You can be a little formal and a little informal, giving your audience reliable information or inspiring messages while also being engaging and memorable.

Next, during your presentation, of course, you need to look the part. So, your dress code plays an important role in the visuals of your audience. The dress code would depend on the type of presentation you are giving and the type of audience you are presenting to. Wearing flashy clothes may distract your audience and make them unable to understand your message.

Another important thing to take note of would be the allotted time for your presentations. Sometimes you will only be given a few minutes to present a huge chunk of information. If a presentation takes too long, you start to lose your audience. But, if it's too short, you might have missed some vital key points. Maximize your time by keeping it short and simple, but without jeopardizing the quality and content of your presentation.

We all know that things may not always go according to plan, but that shouldn’t discourage you from giving a good presentation. You should always be prepared with a backup plan or be prepared to adapt to the situation. For online presentations, a lot can go wrong.

The most common problem would be technical difficulties such as lagging audio or visuals, mic feedback, and so on. Don’t panic, just try to provide a quick fix so that you can continue your presentation. Always try to be calm and collected. If the fix is taking too long, you can try to stall and engage with your audience.

Another possible difficulty would be unexpected interruptions from your audiences, especially from panelists. Panelists may ask questions while you are still presenting. It may disrupt your trail of thought, but you have to persevere. If you need time to answer, you can ask for a minute to think of a sound answer.

One more important thing to take note of for an online presentation, or actually any presentation, is confidence. Confidence will always be key to a successful presentation.

Last but not the least, if your event is sponsored by an organization, it’s a good practice to acknowledge them for giving you the opportunity to make your presentation happen. Thanks to these people, you are able to convey your message to your target audience.

In addition to the tips and tricks mentioned above, there are other factors that can contribute to a successful presentation. One of the most important things to consider is the audience. Knowing your audience and tailoring your presentation to their interests and needs can help you deliver your message effectively.

Another important factor to consider is the use of visual aids. Visual aids such as graphs, charts, and images can help clarify complex information and make your presentation more engaging. However, it’s important to use them sparingly and make sure they are relevant to your presentation.

Practice is also essential to delivering a successful presentation. Practicing your presentation several times can help you feel more confident and comfortable when presenting in front of an audience. You can also ask friends or colleagues to listen to your presentation and provide feedback.

Finally, it’s important to be passionate about your topic. If you are passionate about your topic, it will show in your presentation and help engage your audience. Your enthusiasm can be contagious and inspire others to take an interest in your topic.

In conclusion, delivering a successful presentation takes preparation, practice, and confidence. By following the tips and tricks mentioned above, and considering other factors such as the audience, visual aids, and passion for the topic, you can deliver a memorable and effective presentation. Good luck on your next presentation!
